## IMAGE / LOGO INSTRUCTIONS

**Shared art direction (every mark in the family):** flat line art, single consistent stroke weight, hand-drawn-but-precise. Strictly two colors — warm dark-brown ink `#3E2B22` on warm-gray paper `#D6D1C8`. No gradients, no shading, no text, no gloss, no 3D. Generous negative space, square composition. Each must read at 48–64 px. The point of the family is **one distinct object per product** — boat (studio), the app's object, portcullis (game), lidded pot (memory) — so no two silhouettes collide.

### 6. Combined Stockpot + Butcherblock mark (the memory pair)

From the pot's lid, steam rises and resolves into a small constellation of five to seven linked nodes (a mind map). Reads left-to-right as a pipeline: chop into pieces → into the pot → simmered into connected memory. Single consistent stroke weight, warm dark-brown ink (#3E2B22) on warm gray paper (#D6D1C8), two colors only, no gradients, no text, no gloss. Balanced horizontal composition with generous negative space; should also crop sensibly to a square. Same family as the gravy-boat studio mark.

**How to feed the two finals to the engine:** use both final PNGs as style/reference and instruct it to (1) preserve the exact single stroke weight and the two-color palette, (2) keep each object recognizably itself — do NOT merge the pot and block into one object, (3) compose them into one left-to-right scene with the diced cubes as the connective thread from block to pot, (4) keep the steam→constellation as the apex. Ask for a horizontal lockup plus a square crop.

**Family roll-call check:** gravy boat (studio) · ladle (AppLadle) · portcullis (Keepfall) · lidded pot (Stockpot) · butcher block (Butcherblock) — five distinct silhouettes, one ink, one paper. Stockpot + Butcherblock additionally share the diced-cube motif so the pair reads as a set.
