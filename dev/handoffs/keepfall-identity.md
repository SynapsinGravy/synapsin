# Identity Handoff: Castle Wars → **Keepfall** (working title)

2026-06-12. Decision backed by dedicated collision screening (Play, App Store, Steam, itch.io, BoardGameGeek, trademark and domain registries). Full data: `~/projects/NAMING-AND-BLURBS.md`.

## Your new working title

**Keepfall.** One word: the keep falls — the win condition is the name. Screened **clean everywhere**: no app, game, book, company, or trademark called Keepfall found in any category; `keepfall.app` is unregistered; the only noise is unrelated "Keep Falling" endless-faller arcade games in search results.

## Why not the names that came before

- **"Castle Wars"** is heavily occupied: Jagex's RuneScape Castle Wars minigame owns 20+ years of search SEO; Google Play has exact-name collisions live today (`zogr.castle.wars`, "Castle Wars: Legacy", "Castle Wars Online"); Steam has several more; and the classic XGen Flash card game makes the name evoke the wrong genre entirely.
- **"Marching Order" / "Marching Order: Keepfall"** died in screening despite the perfect mechanical fit: **SFB Games** (Snipperclips, Crow Country — an active, established studio) has sold a game named exactly "Marching Order" on Google Play AND the App Store since ~2016 — same store, same category, strong senior common-law rights, discoverability near zero, and a Play impersonation flag is plausible. Worse for the platform idea: **Crumbling Keep's tabletop RPG line already uses the exact "Marching Order: <Subtitle>" pattern** (Onyx Tower, Curse and Coin) in an adjacent fantasy/party-order genre — future "Marching Order: X" titles would read as their expansions. A subtitle doesn't cure the base name.
- **Platform-pattern note for the future:** if a "<Platform>: <Title>" family is still wanted for later iterations, the platform base name must be screened fresh before use ("Gatefall" is also taken — Grim Rabbit's board game). The simpler pattern that needs no clearance: the studio is the platform — "Keepfall, a Synapsin Gravy Workshop game" (the Tapbots/Simogo model).

Expect reviewers to mention *Thronefall* (well-known minimalist castle-defense, same noun+"fall" construction). That's legally distinct, not confusingly similar — and frankly good company for positioning.

## Identity for all copy

What it is: single-player real-time lane combat — one narrow lane between two castles, deployment order matters because nobody can overtake, breach the gate, get past the King, capture the Princess before they capture yours. 90 seconds to 3 minutes a match. Twelve GOAP AI personalities that play under identical rules — difficulty is tuned economically, never by cheating. 36-battle campaign. Godot 4.4, Android-first.
